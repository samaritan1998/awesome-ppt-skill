# Academic Mode

Use for papers, thesis defenses, literature reports, lab meetings, and research updates.

## Structure

1. Title and context.
2. Problem and motivation.
3. Prior work or baseline.
4. Method or system design.
5. Experiment setup.
6. Main results.
7. Ablations or analysis.
8. Limitations.
9. Conclusion and next steps.

## Rules

- Preserve claim-source mapping.
- Avoid overloading slides with full paper paragraphs.
- Convert dense tables into the smallest readable comparison.
- Keep formulas only when they are essential to the argument.
- Explain axes, metrics, datasets, and baselines.
- Put detailed citations or extra derivations in notes or appendix slides.
